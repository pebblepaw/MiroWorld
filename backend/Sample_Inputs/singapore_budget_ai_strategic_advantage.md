# Singapore Budget 2025: Harness AI as a Strategic Advantage

Source: https://www.singaporebudget.gov.sg/budget-speech/budget-statement/c-harness-ai-as-a-strategic-advantage#Harness-AI-as-a-Strategic-Advantage

This document summarizes the Singapore Budget section on using artificial intelligence as a strategic advantage for Singapore. It is intended as the default demo policy input for knowledge extraction, population sampling, simulation, and report generation.

## Policy Intent

Singapore frames AI as a national strategic capability rather than a standalone technology trend. The policy position is that AI should raise productivity, unlock discovery, and help Singapore overcome structural constraints such as limited natural resources, an ageing population, and a tight labor market. The government also acknowledges public concerns about job displacement, misinformation, bias, and unsafe or unethical AI deployment.

The overall stance is that Singapore should not try to build the largest frontier models. Instead, it should focus on deploying AI quickly, responsibly, and effectively in areas where the country already has institutional strength.

## Main Policy Measures

### 1. National AI Missions

Singapore will launch national AI Missions to drive AI-led transformation in four sectors:

- Advanced manufacturing
- Connectivity and logistics
- Finance
- Healthcare

These missions are expected to have clear objectives and measurable outcomes rather than open-ended experimentation.

### 2. Regulatory and Coordination Changes

The government plans to review regulations and create sandboxes so companies can test AI innovations safely and responsibly. It also plans to align research and development, regulation, and investment-promotion efforts across agencies so that public institutions act in a coordinated way.

### 3. National AI Council

A new National AI Council will provide strategic direction and oversee Singapore's AI agenda at the national level.

### 4. Enterprise Transformation Support

The policy emphasizes that AI adoption is difficult because firms must organize data, rebuild systems, redesign jobs, and retrain workers. To support deeper transformation:

- A new Champions of AI programme will support firms that want to use AI to transform their businesses comprehensively.
- Support will include enterprise transformation and workforce training.

### 5. SME and Broad-Based Adoption Support

For wider business adoption, especially among SMEs:

- The Enterprise Innovation Scheme will be expanded so AI expenditures qualify for support in Years of Assessment 2027 and 2028, capped at 50,000 Singapore dollars per year.
- The Productivity Solutions Grant will be expanded to support a broader range of digital and AI-enabled solutions.

The policy narrative uses local businesses as examples of firms that can improve operations, customer service, and revenue through practical AI adoption.

### 6. AI Ecosystem Development

Singapore plans to build on the Lorong AI pilot and establish a larger AI park at one-north. The aim is to create a focal point where founders, researchers, practitioners, and enterprises can collaborate and translate ideas into deployable solutions.

## Workforce and Social Implications

The policy explicitly recognizes workforce anxiety around AI. It presents the government's responsibility as twofold:

- Help enterprises adopt AI productively
- Help workers adjust, reskill, and benefit from the transition

This makes the policy highly relevant to workers in services, logistics, transport, healthcare, education, finance, and manufacturing, as well as to SMEs that may struggle with transformation costs.

Likely areas of public debate include:

- Whether AI-driven productivity gains will be shared fairly
- Whether mid-career and lower-wage workers will benefit from retraining
- Whether SMEs can realistically absorb the cost of adoption
- Whether governance and safety rules are strong enough
- Whether public-sector coordination can keep pace with fast-moving technology change

## Stakeholders Likely to Care Most

- Workers facing automation or workflow redesign
- Mid-career workers needing retraining
- SME owners and managers adopting AI tools
- Large enterprises pursuing end-to-end AI transformation
- Public agencies coordinating industrial and workforce policy
- Residents concerned about misinformation, bias, and AI governance
